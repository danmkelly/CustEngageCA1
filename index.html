Design Note Agent Selection Rationale

Three agents were selected from the five archetypes. Maker was excluded because this CA is an analytical and design exercise, not a software build. Manager was excluded to keep the pipeline inspectable and failure-rich: manual orchestration exposes errors, hallucinations, and broken handoffs needed for the Failure Dossier. The chosen three (Researcher, Designer, Communicator) map directly onto the trust and transparency lens: intelligence → design → customer-facing language.

Step 3: Designer Execution

The Designer converted the Researcher's Trust Landscape Audit into three buildable intervention specifications, with dark-pattern removal and calibration moves.

Readiness Check Designer, are you clear on your next steps?

Prompt: Designer, are you able to read Researcher's output, and are you clear on your next steps. Please reply here only (not action), with a summary of any concerns and your planned next steps.
Response: The Designer correctly identified the three design specs to produce, raised five concerns (evidence gap on dark patterns, system vs. screen tension, missing competitive context, Fogg B=MAP integration without user data), and escalated one decision: "Do you want me to proceed with assumptions (marked), or pause and request that the Researcher close these gaps first?"

Execution Prompt (Risks Accepted)

Prompt: Great. Please do execute. You can proceed without closure on the open data gaps, but do note your concerns in your output. For output store your handoff as an HTML page (HO-02-Designer-to-Communicator.html), and also create another HTML page (Reflections-Designer.html) within which you will describe your thought process, record data sources used, lay out any tough or marginal decisions, and flag where you may have made errors. Upon completion, please echo this prompt back along with your notes on prompt completion so I can get a screen-grab of prompt & response.

Risk Accepted Known Data Gaps

The human orchestrator explicitly waived closure on the Researcher's five open data gaps, instructing the Designer to proceed with assumptions and flag them. This decision is a key discussion point for the Failure Dossier: it prioritised pipeline velocity over evidence verification.

Step 4: Communicator Execution

The Communicator filled all copy slots from the Designer's specs and produced a business-leadership-facing Final Report with recommendations, options not pursued, and implementation roadmap.

Readiness Check Communicator, are you clear on your next steps?

Prompt: Communicator, are you able to read Designer's output, and are you clear on your next steps. Please reply here only (not action), with a summary of any concerns and your planned next steps.
Response: The Communicator correctly identified 35+ copy slots to fill across three designs, raised five concerns (inability to verify claims, no passenger vocabulary data, single register across touchpoints, chatbot greeting length, confirming dark-pattern assumptions), and planned a register-first, slot-by-slot approach with five quality standards. Readiness confirmed.

Execution Prompt (Concerns Accepted)

Prompt: Thank you. Please do execute. You can proceed without closure on your concerns, but do note your concerns in your output. For output store your handoff as an HTML page (HO-03-Communicator-to-FinalReport.html), and also create another HTML page (Reflections-Communicator.html) within which you will describe your thought process, record data sources used, lay out any tough or marginal decisions, and flag where you may have made errors. The FinalReport should be authored with a business leadership audience in mind - they care to learn of crisp recommendations (with rationale & concerns highlighted), options not pursued (and why), with a focus on business results (not AI usage in creation of this report). Success is a CEO loving the clarity of the report, but with clarity that it was AI generated. Upon completion, please echo this prompt back along with your notes on prompt completion so I can get a screen-grab of prompt & response.

Concerns Waived Unverifiable Claims

The human orchestrator again waived closure on concerns, instructing the Communicator to proceed and flag assumptions. Eight unverified claims were documented in the output, with four marked as critical path for Phase 1 audit. The Final Report was structured with CEO-facing business sections first (Executive Summary, Recommendations, Options Not Pursued, Implementation Roadmap) and detailed copy specifications as the implementation appendix.
